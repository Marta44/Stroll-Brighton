My Tips...

Welcome to Brighton! I am Marta, your personal tourist guide. I would like to give you directions and suggestions while you are strolling one of the most picturesque and artistic cities of the UK. Brighton and Hove welcomes over 9.5 million visitors annually and has been regularly listed in the top five cities that tourists wish to visit during their stay in the UK.
Its vibrant, colorful and there are thousands of places to discover even if you are a local…ready to pace up and down?

Royal Pavilion

The UK Taj Mahal. Is the spectacular and exotic palace of the Prince Regent, King George IV. Completed by the architect John Nash in 1823 became immediately the icon of Brighton for its wonderful mixture of styles: Indian outside and Chinese inside. You can book your tickets here.

Brighton Museum

It is located in the Royal Pavilion’s renovated stable block and its collection ranges from 20th century art, design, and fashion to contemporary art with a focus on artists such as: Pablo Picasso, Eric Ravilious, Grayson Perry and Salvador Dalì. Plan your visit here.

Graffiti

With its graffiti Brighton looks like an Open Air museum. Walk in Kensington Street, North Laine, London Road or in Hanningtons Lane to spot a stunning Alice in Wonderland mural. Did you know that Lewis Carroll used to visit Brighton as his sister lived on Sussex Square?

Colorful Streets

Brighton is famous for its colorful streets. You can spot the most pretty houses in Kemp street, Albion Hill, Gardner Street, Blaker Street, Lynton Street.

Devil's Dyke

The South Downs National Park covers an area of 1,645 square km between Winchester and Eastbourne and is the third largest national park with 39 million visitors a year. The 19th-century landscape painter John Constable thought the panorama from Devil’s Dyke to be “The greatest view in the world”!.

The Prince Albert Pub

On its walls in 2004 the street artist Banksy made a stencil graffiti: the iconic “Kissing Coppers”. It has been sold in 2014 at an auction in Miami and now the one on The Prince Albert pub’s wall is its reproduction. Visit The Prince Albert to watch some good gigs.

The Heart & Hand Pub

One among the many Brighton's traditional pubs. It is famous for its old school vinyl jukebox and is home to a local celebrity : "Bailey" the pub lovely cat.

English's of Brighton restaurant

In the heart of the Lanes there is the oldest seafood restaurant of Brighton. The Leigh-Jones family have been here since 1945. On its walls you can see signed photographs of Charlie Chaplin, Lily Savage and Judi Dench among other celebrities. Book a table here.

Seven Sisters & Eastbourne lighthouse

Only half an hour drive from Brighton the Seven Sisters are a magnificent stretch of white chalk cliffs. A stunning place for outdoor activities such as hiking, canoeing, cycling, bird watching. If you simply want to relax go to Saltmarsh cafe and rooms.

Brighton Marine Palace & Pier

Opened in 1899 the Victorian pier is now one the main attraction for both children and adults. It boasts over 50 gaming machines including air hockey, dance mats, multiplayer games, a ghost train and roller-coasters. Find out more here.

British Airways i360

If you are looking for adrenaline and a breathtaking view, glide up to the 162 meters high tower and enjoy a drink from the South Coast highest bar, the Nyetimber Sky Bar. Since its first flight in 2016 this is slimmest tall tower in the world! Fly up to i360!

Sea Life Centre

Dive into the world’s oldest aquarium! Designed by Eugenius Birch, the same architect of the West Pier, it opened in 1872 and hosts over 5,000 sea creatures. Discover more here.
